## About the Final Project

The Final Project is a **group capstone** (groups of four, randomly assigned) culminating in a **research poster** and a post-conference **Poster-to-Product** build sprint. Across the term your group completes milestones **M00 → M12** (numbering follows the reference documents and skips M04/M07/M13); each member also submits a confidential intra-group peer evaluation at the end. The project is worth **35%** of the course grade — Milestone Deliverables 40%, Peer Evaluation 20%, Instructor/TA Evaluation (poster + Poster-to-Product) 40%. **Presentation at the Fall 2026 Purdue Undergraduate Research Conference (Tue Nov 17) is required for all students.** Full schedule: [`final_project_milestone_reference.md`](final_project_milestone_reference.md).

## Overview

**At least one member from each team** must create and publish a **LinkedIn post** inviting their professional and personal network to attend the group's poster presentation at the Undergraduate Research Conference.

## Requirements for the Post

Include, exactly as listed in the official conference program:

- **Poster Title**
- **Team Members**
- **Date and Time of Presentation**
- **Location (building and room/space)**

Additionally:
- Write in a professional tone suitable for a public audience.
- Make clear you are presenting as part of the Undergraduate Research Conference at Purdue University.
- Encourage your network to attend and support your presentation.
- You may include visuals (team photo, project logo, a graphic of the poster).
- You can tag the instructor: <https://www.linkedin.com/in/davimoreira/>.

## Submission
Upload a screenshot of the LinkedIn post.
