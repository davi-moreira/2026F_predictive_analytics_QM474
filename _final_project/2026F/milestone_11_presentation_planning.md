## Overview

Practice planning and delivering an effective poster presentation: synthesize your main points, identify your audience, and develop **30-second, 90-second, and 2-minute** elevator pitches to adapt to different scenarios. Also prepare strategies for inviting questions and for engaging the audience with your own.

## Instructions

### 1. Identify Your Target Audience
A one-paragraph summary of who your audience will be (peers, domain experts, general public) and **two or three key points** that resonate with them (career relevance, societal impact, technical novelty).

### 2. Prepare Elevator Pitches
- **30-second pitch** — grab attention; project title, main question/hypothesis, significance. *Script ≤ 75 words.*
- **90-second pitch** — add brief methodology + key results, and why they matter. *Script ≤ 200 words.*
- **2-minute pitch** — cohesive overview: intro, methodology, results, interpretation, next steps; include real-world application. *Script ≤ 300 words.*

### 3. Poster Integration
Note on the poster where you will visually reference key charts/tables, methodological steps, and conclusions; provide a short bullet outline of how you transition between poster sections.

### 4. Question & Discussion Strategy
- **Inviting questions:** when and how you will prompt the audience (e.g., *"Do you see other potential applications?"*).
- **Asking questions:** prepare **≥3 questions** to ask your audience that inspire critical thinking or draw connections.

### 5. Submission Format
A written PDF containing: (1) one-paragraph audience analysis; (2) the three elevator-pitch scripts; (3) poster-reference outline; (4) question & discussion strategy (≥3 audience-directed questions).

## Assessment Criteria
- Clarity and conciseness of pitches — **30%**
- Appropriateness for the identified audience — **20%**
- Effectiveness of poster integration — **20%**
- Creativity/thoroughness of the question-invitation strategy — **15%**
- Overall presentation cohesion and professionalism — **15%**
